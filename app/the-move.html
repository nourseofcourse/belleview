A section of the historic Belleview Biltmore Hotel prepares to move into the 21-century

Belleview Inn (link to BI page on main website), the renovated and reimagined lobby and 35 guest rooms of the original Belleview Biltmore Hotel, is set become the centerpiece of the new gated community of Belleview Place as a boutique hotel and amenity center. The 38,000-square-foot structure will be rotated 90 degrees and transported 320 feet to its new foundation at the top of brick-surfaced Belleview Boulevard.

The preparation process has begun and will continue through mid-December, when the move will take place over two days.

The Pour

Continuous pouring of concrete for the foundation of Belleview Inn ran from 2 a.m. to 9 a.m. Dec. 8, with fine tuning throughout the day. The pour included 500 cubic yards of concrete — more than 50 truckloads and went off without a glitch.

The Move

The relocation process includes 47 eight-wheeled computerized hydraulic dollies, which allows one person to operate the entire raising process. It ensures that each jack receives the same volume of oil and extends at the same rate, regardless of the weight or pressure on it.
