Belleair Country Club

Mere steps across the private road that circles Belleview Place lies the incomparable Belleair Country Club, which claims the oldest golf course in Florida. Originally part of the Belleview Biltmore Hotel resort, the prestigious Club lives up to its history and reputation, and is only the beginning of the treasures at your doorstep. Even better: The initiation fee to the Club is included in the purchase of your Belleview Place home.

reserve tour

Golf

Established in 1897 with six holes, the course was expanded and redesigned as two 18-hole courses in 1915 by Donald J. Ross. Among the few waterfront links in the area, the par-71 courses are favored by golfers at all levels. A driving range, two putting greens and knowledgeable pros round out your golf experience.

Sports Center & Spa

Health and wellness take center stage at the 10,000-square-foot fitness facility with state-of-the-art machines, 140-plus monthly classes, licensed massage therapists and aestheticians, plus a sports shop, and playrooms and camps for kids, locker rooms and snack bar.

Dining

The clubhouse features both casual and formal dining and lounge options, with delightful fare and spectacular panoramas over fairways and greens — or more intimate and beautifully lit evening views toward Clearwater Bay. Meeting rooms for various groups offer convenience and superb service.

Tennis, Pickleball, Croquet

Seven well-lighted Har-Tru tennis courts help hone skills and build friendships late into the evening. The fast-growing sport of pickleball (with elements of tennis, badminton and ping-pong) also finds a home at Belleair, while a croquet court carries on a long tradition.

Swimming

Lessons, competitions, fitness, swim camps and good old-fashioned splashing-good fun find a home in the Club’s heated and cooled junior Olympic-size pool and nearby family fun pool. The outdoor pool center includes a healthy balance of sun and shade and an all-important snack shop.
